The beta app runs on Apple’s TestFlight & Google’s internal-testing track, which means every purchase — Monthly, Annual, Lifetime, or Starter — goes through the store’s sandbox environment. 01

Install the app

Pick your phone

We send invites two ways: a direct link to your inbox, and a public link you can share with anyone we’ve cleared. Either one will drop you into the store flow below.

iOS

via TestFlight
  1. Install TestFlight from the App Store
    It’s Apple’s free beta-testing app. Search TestFlight in the App Store and install it. Sign in with the Apple ID you want to use.
  2. Open the invite link on your phone
    You’ll receive a TestFlight invite from us either by email (tap View in TestFlight) or as a public link we share directly. Open whichever you were sent — it launches straight into TestFlight. Don’t have one yet? Email helloetapa@gmail.com and we’ll send one.
  3. Accept and install Etapa
    Tap Accept, then Install. The beta build downloads alongside your App Store apps — with a small orange dot next to the icon so you can tell it apart.
  4. Open Etapa and sign in
    Use the email address your invite was sent to. You’ll land on the home screen — ready to ride.
Request TestFlight invite Get TestFlight

Android

via Google Play internal testing
  1. Send us the email on your Google account
    We need to add it to our tester list before you can install. Email helloetapa@gmail.com with the Google address tied to your Play Store.
  2. Accept the tester invitation
    You’ll get an email from Google Play. Tap Become a tester. This opts you into the internal build — no sideloading, no settings changes.
  3. Open the download link
    The same email has a link to Etapa on the Play Store. Open it on your phone and tap Install. The listing shows an “Internal testing” badge so you know you’ve got the right build.
  4. Open Etapa and sign in
    Same email as your tester invite. Future updates install automatically, like any other Play Store app.

02

What to test

Six focus areas

You don’t need to cover everything. Pick the areas that match how you’d actually use Etapa and go deep. Every note helps — even “this felt fine” is data.

Start here

Onboarding & goal setup

The first five minutes decide whether a new rider stays. This is the highest-signal area to test.

  • Does anything feel confusing, unnecessary, or too “coach-y”?
  • Were any questions hard to answer honestly?
  • Did the generated plan match what you told us?
Core loop

Your plan & the week ahead

This is the screen most riders will open daily. It needs to be glanceable and trustworthy.

  • Can you tell at a glance what today’s session is?
  • Is moving or skipping a session obvious?
  • Does the week feel realistic for your schedule?
The voice

Coach chat

Ask your coach anything — session advice, nutrition, recovery, pacing. Push it and see where it breaks.

  • Did the answers sound like a real, helpful person?
  • Any jargon slip through that needs plain English?
  • Did it ever feel preachy, generic, or off-tone?
Connections

Strava sync

Connect Strava from Settings, then go for a ride. Check that it appears in Etapa.

  • Did the connection flow work first time?
  • Did your ride show up, with correct distance & time?
  • Any rides missing or duplicated?
Paywall

Plans & purchase flow

Please do try buying a plan — the sandbox makes it free, but we need eyes on the whole flow.

  • Is the pricing clear at a glance? Which tier felt best value?
  • Did the Apple / Google purchase sheet work end-to-end?
  • Anything about the page that made you want to close it?
  • Once “purchased”, did the app unlock correctly?

Reminder: payments are sandbox — no real money moves.

Reliability

Bugs & crashes

Anything that feels broken, slow, or wrong. Screenshots & the moment it happened are gold.

  • App crashes or freezes
  • Loading states that never finish
  • Text overflow, cut-off buttons, wonky layout
Tone

Copy & feel

Etapa is supposed to read like a knowledgeable friend. Tell us where it doesn’t.

  • Sentences that felt robotic or corporate
  • Anything intimidating for a first-time cyclist
  • Button labels that made you pause

Where to send feedback

Two channels, same inbox

Whichever is closer to hand. Both land with the same small team, and we read everything.

Send it the way that fits the moment.

If you’ve just hit something in the app, use the in-app option — you’ll have the context still in your head. For bigger thoughts, longer screenshots, or anything you want to attach a file to, email works best.

In the app
Settings → Send Feedback
