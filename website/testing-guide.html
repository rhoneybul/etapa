Installing Etapa (first time)

  1. Send us your Google account email
    Google Play internal testing is invite-only — only whitelisted Google accounts can see the listing. Email helloetapa@gmail.com with the exact Google address your Play Store is signed in with. This is usually a Gmail address but can be any Google-linked email — whichever one shows up in Play Store → profile icon (top right) → “Manage accounts”. Adding you takes a couple of minutes on our side.
  2. Wait ~5 minutes after we confirm you're added
    Google Play caches tester lists aggressively. If you open the install link before Google has propagated the change you'll get a “not available” error even though everything is set up correctly. Five minutes is almost always enough; up to 30 in rare cases.
  3. Open the Play Store link on your phone
    Tap https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.etapa.app on the Android device you want to test on. The listing must show an “Internal testing” label under the app name — that's the confirmation you're looking at the tester build. If you only see a normal Play Store listing without that badge, you're either on the wrong Google account or the propagation delay hasn't finished.
  4. Tap Install
    Standard Play Store install — download, then Open. No sideloading, no developer settings, no unknown-sources warnings. The first launch can take a few extra seconds while Android verifies the install.
  5. Open Etapa and sign in
    Use the email address your invite was sent to. You’ll land on the home screen — ready to ride.

Getting updates

  1. Updates install automatically in the background
    As long as Play Store auto-update is on (default), new builds roll out silently within a few hours of us pushing them. You don't need to do anything. You'll just notice the app has restarted and maybe a new feature appeared.
  2. To force an update right now
    Open the install link again. If there's a newer version available, the primary button changes from “Open” to “Update”. Alternatively: Play Store → profile icon → Manage apps & device → Updates available → find Etapa.
  3. Check which version you're running
    In the app: Settings → About shows the version + build number. In Play Store: the listing scrolls down to “About this app” → version. If we've asked you to reproduce a bug and the build numbers don't match, try forcing an update first.
  4. Something feels broken after an update?
    Usually a stale cache. Force-close Etapa (recent apps → swipe up on Etapa), then re-open. If the problem persists, Settings (device) → Apps → Etapa → Storage → Clear cache. Don't tap “Clear storage” unless we ask — that signs you out and loses any locally-queued data.

Common Android problems

  • “App not available for your device” or “Item not found”

    Your Google account isn't on the tester list yet, or the propagation hasn't finished. Confirm with us that the email address is right and wait 5–30 minutes. If you recently changed the Google account your Play Store uses, you may also need to log out and back in of the Play Store app.

  • Install button is greyed out or says “Not compatible”

    Etapa requires Android 7.0+ (Nougat) and a reasonably modern processor. If you're on Android 6 or earlier, or a very old device, we can't install. Check: Settings (device) → About phone → Android version. Any device from 2018 or later will work.

  • Multiple Google accounts on the phone

    Play Store defaults to the primary account. If you asked us to add a secondary Google account, switch to it explicitly: Play Store → profile icon → account switcher → pick the one we whitelisted. Then re-open the install link.

  • Uninstalled Etapa — can I reinstall?

    Yes, straight from the same install link. Your tester status stays active even after uninstalling, so there's nothing to re-accept.

  • Stuck on a particular screen, or the app crashes

    We want to know. In the app: Settings → Send feedback (a screenshot is included automatically). Or email helloetapa@gmail.com with a screenshot and the build number.

02

What to test

Six focus areas

You don’t need to cover everything. Pick the areas that match how you’d actually use Etapa and go deep. Every note helps — even “this felt fine” is data.

Start here

Onboarding & goal setup

The first five minutes decide whether a new rider stays. This is the highest-signal area to test.

  • Does anything feel confusing, unnecessary, or too “coach-y”?
  • Were any questions hard to answer honestly?
  • Did the generated plan match what you told us?
Core loop

Your plan & the week ahead

This is the screen most riders will open daily. It needs to be glanceable and trustworthy.

  • Can you tell at a glance what today’s session is?
  • Is moving or skipping a session obvious?
  • Does the week feel realistic for your schedule?
The voice

Coach chat

Ask your coach anything — session advice, nutrition, recovery, pacing. Push it and see where it breaks.

  • Did the answers sound like a real, helpful person?
  • Any jargon slip through that needs plain English?
  • Did it ever feel preachy, generic, or off-tone?
Connections

Strava sync

Connect Strava from Settings, then go for a ride. Check that it appears in Etapa.

  • Did the connection flow work first time?
  • Did your ride show up, with correct distance & time?
  • Any rides missing or duplicated?
Paywall

Plans & purchase flow

Please do try buying a plan — the sandbox makes it free, but we need eyes on the whole flow.

  • Is the pricing clear at a glance? Which tier felt best value?
  • Did the Apple / Google purchase sheet work end-to-end?
  • Anything about the page that made you want to close it?
  • Once “purchased”, did the app unlock correctly?

Reminder: payments are sandbox — no real money moves.

Reliability

Bugs & crashes

Anything that feels broken, slow, or wrong. Screenshots & the moment it happened are gold.

  • App crashes or freezes
  • Loading states that never finish
  • Text overflow, cut-off buttons, wonky layout
Tone

Copy & feel

Etapa is supposed to read like a knowledgeable friend. Tell us where it doesn’t.

  • Sentences that felt robotic or corporate
  • Anything intimidating for a first-time cyclist
  • Button labels that made you pause
