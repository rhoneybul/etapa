# Etapa — Instagram Posting Plan

**Drafted:** 17 April 2026
**Horizon:** 10 weeks from first post → gets you to launch readiness
**Inputs:** 15 drafted image briefs in `website/blog/ads/`, brand DNA in `BRAND.md`, this runs in parallel with the SEO content calendar in `SEO_CONTENT_STRATEGY.md`.

---

## 1. Principles

1. **Audience-first, SEO-second.** Instagram is for awareness and audience affinity. Every post should either (a) make a beginner feel seen or (b) teach them one useful thing. The blog link is the quiet CTA, not the point of the post.
2. **Emotional → practical → brand, on repeat.** The cadence that compounds is: one post that makes people *feel*, one post that makes people *learn*, one post that reminds them *who we are*. Don't stack two practical posts back-to-back.
3. **Brand intro every ~2 weeks.** New followers need context. A pinned brand-introduction post plus a re-introduction post every 10–14 days keeps the "what is Etapa / who is it for" front-and-centre without boring regulars.
4. **Show the AI, always.** Every post carries the *Made with AI · Etapa* chip. Pinned posts and brand re-intros say it out loud in copy, not just in the corner chip.
5. **Post when beginners are online.** Target windows (UK time): Tue/Wed/Thu 7:00–8:30am (pre-work ride-fantasy window), Sun 10:00–11:30am (Sunday-ride planning window). Weekends outperform for beginner audiences.

---

## 2. Recommended cadence

**Posting volume:** **4 posts / week** for weeks 1–4, then **3 / week** from week 5 onward once paid ad budget starts carrying the repetition load.

Per week, the mix is:

- **1× emotional / audience-affinity post** (UGC or image with a human hook)
- **1× practical / how-to post** (Avatar or image card)
- **1× stat / quote / micro-insight** (image only — cheapest to produce, highest saves)
- **1× brand or progress moment** (product shot, pinned update, or brand re-intro every 2 weeks)

Format split across a week:

- 2× static images (feed or carousel)
- 1× Reel (Avatar or UGC)
- 1× story-series (repurposed from the week's strongest post — 3–5 frames)

This rhythm lets the blog-driven briefs (the 15 already in `/ads`) do the heavy lifting while keeping one "brand" slot free every week.

## 3. Post-zero — the pinned brand intro

Before Week 1 goes live, **produce and pin the brand-introduction post** (we drafted the copy for this in the previous session). Keep it pinned for the full 10 weeks. This is the post new followers see first and it must be unambiguous:

- *Who* Etapa is for (beginners, improvers, women returning to the sport)
- *What* it does (AI training plan + AI coach)
- *Why* it's different (not for racers, upfront about AI, built for the step before "proper cyclist")

Format: AI image ad, 9:16, Etapa icon or single rider at dawn, on-creative headline "Cycling, for people who weren't always cyclists."

## 5. Brand-promotion cadence summary

| Type | Frequency | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Pinned brand intro | 1× at start, left for 10 weeks | Post #1 |
| Brand re-intro posts | Every ~2 weeks | Posts #12, #20, #31 |
| Product/feature moments | Every ~3 weeks | Week 6 screen-recording, coach spotlight series in weeks 8–10 |

Rule of thumb: **1 brand post in every 5–6 posts** early on, tapering to 1 in 7–8 once you have consistent audience retention.

## 6. Pinned-post rotation

Pinning is a strong signal — update it monthly to match where your audience is:

- **Month 1 (weeks 1–4):** Brand intro post
- **Month 2 (weeks 5–8):** Best-performing UGC post from month 1 (most likely `learn-cycling-as-an-adult` or `cycling-gatekeeping-toxic-culture`)
- **Month 3 (weeks 9–10 + into launch):** Launch runway post / offer post

---

## 7. Stories strategy

Stories run in parallel to feed, not as a replacement:

- Every feed post → 3-frame story repost within 24h (hook / quote / swipe-up to blog)
- One **"ride of the week"** story series per week — repurposed from Strava screenshots or rider submissions, tagged with coach and plan context
- Q&A stickers on Sundays — answered live by a team member (or as Avatar video) the following Tuesday. Beginner questions are gold for content ideation.
- Keep the AI-transparency chip on every story too

---

## 8. Reels vs static — what to lean on

Reels deliver 2–3× more reach than static on a new account. Prioritise:

- **Week 1–4:** 1 Reel / week minimum
- **Week 5 onward:** 2 Reels / week (the coach spotlight series in weeks 8–10 will naturally do this)

UGC and Avatar briefs are both Reel-native. Image briefs can be converted to Reels by animating a single frame (subtle zoom + captions) — cheap and effective for stats/quote posts.

---

## 9. Paid amplification

The 15 image briefs double as paid-ad creative. Suggested paid ramp:

- **Weeks 1–2:** Organic only. Learn what resonates.
- **Week 3:** Boost the top-performing organic post (budget: £10–20/day for 3 days) targeted at beginners 25–50, UK, cycling + adjacent interests.
- **Week 5 onward:** Always-on £20–50/day on the 2 best-performing ads, rotated monthly.

Track: link clicks, blog time-on-page (via UTM → GA), app install attributions.

## 12. Metrics to watch

Weekly review on Mondays:

- Follower count (absolute + growth rate)
- Reach per post (Reels vs static)
- Saves + shares — the single best signal of beginner resonance (more predictive than likes)
- Link clicks + UTM'd blog sessions
- Top 3 posts by saves → these become next month's boosted ads
- Comments quality (not just count) — watch for beginner-specific language
