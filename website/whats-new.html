26 April 2026

Faster setup, fewer taps, nothing lost in transit.

First open

You meet your coach the second you arrive — not three screens later

The onboarding tour now fires the moment you finish signing in, before you see an empty home screen wondering what to do. Three steps, then you're in.

Picking a coach and choosing km or miles aren't optional any more — those two answers shape the rest of the app, so we ask once and remember forever. Skip is gone from those steps; the Continue button stays disabled with a clear "Pick a coach to continue" hint until you've chosen.

Plan setup

Recommendations that adapt to where you are and what you picked

On the training-types and build-your-week steps you now get a small "we typically recommend" card — and the recommendation actually changes based on your fitness level and the session types you've chosen so far. Conservative for beginners, with the right tail extras when you've added intervals or hills.

We've also stopped asking the same thing twice. If you picked a coach in onboarding, you don't have to pick again here. If your plan picker review was final, we don't bounce you through a second confirm screen. Going back from a later step actually goes back, instead of dropping you on Home.

Home screen

More of your week, on one screen, without scrolling

Today and Tomorrow used to take a full vertical column each. They now sit in a compact horizontal scroll showing the next five days, so the week feels like a real week instead of two looming hero cards.

"+ New plan" shrunk too — it's a small circular button in the YOUR PLANS header now, not a chunky pill that pushed everything down. The full pill only appears when you have no plans yet, where it actually belongs.

Calendar

Drag a session right from the calendar grid

Press-and-hold any day in the calendar grid that has a ride on it and you can drag the session straight to a new date — no need to scroll down to the day list and pick it up from there.

Same gesture, same press-and-hold timing as Home. Works on any cell that has a draggable activity (we pick the first ride if there are multiple).

Coach chat

Nothing you send goes missing — even if you close the app mid-reply

We rewrote how chat is stored end-to-end. Every message — sent, received, in flight, or failed — lives in your local cache so the conversation re-opens instantly and looks the way you left it.

If a coach reply is still being generated when you switch away, we pick it back up when you return. And if a message of yours fails to send, the resend icon is now a small circular arrow inside the message bubble itself — exactly where you'd expect to tap.

Notifications

Tap a notification, land exactly where it points

A coach-reply or coach check-in tap now opens the chat scrolled to the exact message that triggered it — no hunting through history. Plan-ready taps drop you straight into the new plan overview.

The Notifications screen also reads from cache first, so it pops open instantly with the latest list and refreshes in the background — a small "Refreshing…" hint sits in the header while we check.

Polish pass

Smaller things you'll notice without quite noticing

A round of quiet improvements that don't fit a feature card but make the app feel more cared-for end to end.

  • Minimal splash screenJust the icon and a soft pink halo on launch — no more big "ETAPA / train with purpose" text. The native splash now matches the in-app loader exactly.
  • Settings doesn't jump on loadThe skeleton holds the same shape as the loaded screen, so rows don't pop into existence — they just fill in.
  • Coach commits to changes properlyIf your coach says "I'll move that long ride to Sunday", the plan actually moves. We hardened the prompt so commitment language has to come with a structured update.
  • Onboarding tour visible to brand-new accountsThe tour used to hide behind the empty-state Welcome screen on day one. Now it fires immediately, every time.
  • Calendar keyboard avoidanceThe chat input above the keyboard properly lifts on Calendar now, instead of disappearing behind it.
  • Plan picker review goes straight to setupConfirming the review takes you directly into the plan-config flow — the redundant in-between screen is gone.
Bugs squashed
  • User messages no longer disappear after a follow-up — server now uses the live conversation as the baseline before appending the coach's reply.
  • Coach replies stop arriving as bare commitments — "I'll change that" without an actual change is now blocked.
  • Onboarding tour now fires for genuinely new accounts, not just ones with at least one plan.
  • Trial banner now appears for both trialing and starter states, with a sensible fallback when the period-end date is missing.
  • Tapping a day on Home reliably scrolls the matching row into view — we measure at click time now instead of relying on stale layout values.
  • Calendar chat input lifts above the keyboard on Android, instead of being hidden by it.
  • Splash screen on iOS and Android no longer shows the old "ETAPA / train with purpose" text — both now match the minimal in-app loader.
24 April 2026

A smarter home screen, clearer sessions, better plans.

Home screen

Answers "what am I doing today?" the moment you open the app

Your today's session is now the first thing you see — with the ride name, distance, duration, effort, and a one-tap "View details" button. Tomorrow's session sits right beneath it so you can plan your evening.

Below that, the week strip shows every day at a glance with session-type tags (Endurance, Intervals, Long Ride…). Your coach chat lives at the top of the screen so you're never more than a tap away from a question.

Hard sessions

Know exactly what to do — in plain English, heart rate, or watts

"4×4 minutes hard" used to leave a lot of room for guessing. Now every interval or tempo session comes with a proper breakdown: warm-up, main set, cool-down — and the target effort is shown three ways.

Feel it by breathing and legs if you ride by perceived effort. Hit a heart-rate zone if you wear a strap. Match a watt range if you have a power meter. We render whichever you've set — and you always get a plain-English sensory cue so beginners aren't left out.

Pick the right plan

A plan built for where you actually are — in under a minute

Three quick questions: what are you training for, how long is your current longest ride, and when's your event? That's it. No FTP test, no jargon, no "beginner / intermediate / advanced" guesswork.

Your actual longest ride becomes the anchor for Week 1, so your plan starts at a distance you can genuinely complete — and builds up from there.

First open

Meet your coach before you meet your plan

The first time you open Etapa, you'll pick from six coach personalities — from Clara (warm and patient) to Lars (no-nonsense and direct) — and hear a sample of each one's voice before you commit.

We also ask km or miles once, remember it, and show every distance in your preferred unit from then on. About 30 seconds, four taps, done.

Plan picker

Review every answer before the plan is built

After the plan picker's questions you get a summary screen: what you're training for, the kind of cycling you do, your event details, your longest ride, and how long you want to train. Tap any row to change just that one answer — no scrolling back through the flow, no starting over.

Going back from any later screen now returns you to the previous step instead of dumping you on Home. If you notice a mistyped race distance three screens later, one tap gets you back to fix it.

Coach chat

Your coach reads the whole conversation, not just your last line

If your coach asked "recreational swim or something specific?" and you replied "Recreational swim", the old guardrail sometimes flagged that as off-topic. The coach now reads the last few turns of context before deciding, so short answers to its own questions stop getting blocked.

It can also schedule non-cycling sessions — swim, run, physio, mobility, stretching, yoga, and 20+ more — and each one shows with a proper activity icon on your week.

Plans

Plans that match your reality

Beginner plans now properly build toward your target distance without exceeding it before graduation day — so the final ride feels like the milestone it should. We've also tightened progression and added a few guardrails so the AI produces more consistent, more achievable weeks.

  • Your longest ride is now the anchorPlans start where you are, not at a generic benchmark.
  • Beginner target-distance ceilingNo training ride exceeds your target until graduation.
  • Warm-up + cool-down built inEvery hard session comes with a structured breakdown.
  • Max HR and FTP supportAdd yours in Settings to see real bpm / watts on sessions.
  • Shorter plan creationWe stopped asking the same thing twice. Cycling type once on the first question; beginners skip the old pre-quiz entirely.
