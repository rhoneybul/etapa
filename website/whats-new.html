Multi-bike training

Road, gravel, MTB, e-bike, indoor — pick everything you ride

The "what kind of cycling?" question used to make you pick one. Now you tap every bike in your shed — and your plan understands what each one's good for. Long endurance days lean road or gravel. Threshold intervals pick the bike that lets you hold a steady pace. Recovery rides go on whatever's easiest to get out the door on. Bad-weather day? An indoor option will already be in the rotation.

Inside any session, an Edit tap reveals a Bike row with chips for everything you set up. Tap a different chip and we suggest a sensible distance and duration adjustment — same effort, same time, different kilometres — so the workout still does what it's meant to. We held a quick coach Q&A on the right multipliers (gravel ≈ 0.75× the distance, MTB ≈ 0.65×, indoor drops distance entirely, e-bike ≈ 1.05×) and baked them into the suggestion sheet. If you'd rather hold the original 45 km on gravel as a stretch goal, there's a one-tap "Keep original" escape hatch.

And for combos that just don't work — road threshold intervals onto an MTB, say — we say so. Surface noise breaks the stimulus. The sheet flips into a "this swap won't work well" mode with Reschedule / Pick another bike CTAs. Override is still right there if you really mean it; we just won't pretend it's the same session.

Regular rides

Your weekly club ride deserves a kinder form

Adding a Friday club ride used to mean fighting an inline form that scrolled itself off-screen the moment you tapped a field. We pulled that form out into a proper bottom sheet: one focused screen, sticky Save / Cancel buttons that never disappear behind the keyboard, and fields you can actually see while you're typing into them.

Tapping an already-added ride now opens the same sheet, pre-populated with that ride's details — so you can move a club ride from Friday to Saturday or bump the distance from 45 km to 50 km without deleting it and starting over. The little × on the card still removes a ride; the rest of the card is now a tap target for editing. A "Delete this ride" link sits inside the editor too for anyone who looked there first.

If you set up more than one bike type at intake, the sheet also lets you tag a regular ride with a specific bike — handy for "Friday is always the gravel ride."

Plan picker

The keyboard no longer hides the field you're typing into

On the longest-ride question, picking "Enter exact" used to mount the input right where the keyboard was about to appear — so the field you needed to type into vanished behind it. The whole intake flow now lifts above the keyboard the way Activity Detail already does, and the custom-km field scrolls itself into view the moment it mounts. Same fix applied to the event-details step's distance, elevation, and time inputs.

Indoor sessions

Take your indoor session to Zwift, Wahoo SYSTM, Rouvy or TrainerRoad

Open any indoor session and you'll see a new pink "Send to your trainer" card above your coach. Tap it, pick a format, and we generate a real structured workout file — warm-up, intervals, cool-down, with power targets as a percentage of your FTP so the trainer drives resistance for you in ERG mode. We export the same shape the indoor app would have asked you to build by hand.

Two formats:

  • .zwo — the modern standard. Read by Zwift, Wahoo SYSTM, Rouvy, MyWhoosh, TrainerRoad, intervals.icu, and most others.
  • .mrc — universal text-based fallback. Read by CompuTrainer / PerfPro / ErgVideo / GoldenCheetah, plus any other older app that doesn't take .zwo.

Two ways the file actually reaches your trainer app — which path you take depends on the app:

Direct from the share sheet — works today on iOS for apps that registered .zwo as a file type:

  • Zwift (iPad / iPhone) — tap the card → "Zwift / Wahoo SYSTM / Rouvy (.zwo)" → on the share sheet pick Copy to Zwift. The workout appears under Custom Workouts > Etapa next time you fire up Zwift on any device signed into the same account.
  • MyWhoosh — same flow, "Open in MyWhoosh" lands it in your custom workouts.

Save to Files, then import on the web — the path for apps whose mobile clients don't take .zwo directly. Tap Save to Files in the share sheet, drop the file in iCloud Drive, then on the app's web dashboard:

  • Wahoo SYSTM — sign in at systm.wahoofitness.com → Workouts → Import. The session syncs to your phone the next time you open SYSTM.
  • Rouvy — sign in at my.rouvy.com → Workouts → Add → upload the .zwo. Syncs to mobile / desktop on next launch.
  • TrainerRoad — sign in at trainerroad.com → Workouts > Workout Creator > Import.
  • intervals.icu — sign in at intervals.icu → Workouts → Import → drop the .zwo. Free, and it'll then push to a paired Garmin / Wahoo head unit on next sync.
  • CompuTrainer / PerfPro / older apps — choose .mrc instead of .zwo. Same warm-up / main / cool-down shape, plain-text format every legacy app reads.

On Android the flow is "download then web import" for everyone. Most Android trainer apps don't register .zwo as a file type, so Android's share sheet won't usually offer "Open in Zwift" the way iOS does. Tap the export, your browser downloads the file to your phone, then sign in to the app's web dashboard (Zwift, SYSTM, Rouvy, TrainerRoad, intervals.icu) and import from there. Workouts sync to the mobile app on next launch. Etapa shows you a one-time popup explaining this when you first tap the export button — there's a "Don't show this again" checkbox if you'd rather not see it each time.

A few more things worth knowing up front. ERG-mode resistance only works if your trainer app knows your FTP — set it once in the app you ride with, not in Etapa, and every imported workout will play back at the right watts. Garmin Edge and Wahoo ELEMNT head units don't read .zwo directly: route it via Zwift / SYSTM / intervals.icu and let one of those push to the head unit on next sync. Native Garmin Connect Workout export (.fit) and a TrainingPeaks bridge are next on the roadmap.

Privacy note: the export URL is signed with a per-server secret and expires in five minutes. It's scoped to a single (rider, plan, session, format) tuple — it can't be re-used to fetch any other workout, and tampering with the URL (changing the activity id, the user, or the format) fails the signature check.

A clearer way to pick your plan. Drag-and-drop sessions. A chat that never drops a message. A brand-new coach.

Choosing your plan

A plan built around where you actually are — in under a minute

Three quick questions: what are you training for, how long is your current longest ride, and when's your event. That's it. No FTP test, no jargon, no "beginner / intermediate / advanced" guesswork. Your actual longest ride becomes the anchor for Week 1, so your plan starts at a distance you can genuinely complete and builds up from there.

On the training-types and build-your-week steps you'll see a small "we typically recommend" card — and the recommendation adapts to your fitness level and the session types you've already chosen. After your answers, a review screen lays out every choice as a tap-to-edit row — no scrolling back through the flow, no starting over, and going back from any later step actually goes back.

Drag and drop

Move a session straight from home or the calendar

Press-and-hold any session on Home and drag it to a new day. Or open Calendar, press-and-hold any cell that already has a ride on it, and drag the session right out of the grid — no need to scroll down to the day list to pick it up first.

Same gesture, same press-and-hold timing in both places. If you move a session to a day before Week 1 starts, we honour the move without re-generating the rest of the plan around it.

Messaging

A chat that opens instantly and never loses a reply

We rewrote how coach chat is stored end-to-end. Open the screen and your conversation is right there from the cache, then quietly reconciles with the server in the background. Send a message and close the app mid-reply — when you come back, your message and the coach's reply are both intact. If a send fails, a small inline resend arrow appears inside the bubble, exactly where you'd expect to tap.

Push notifications now drop you on the exact message that triggered them, not the top of the chat. Plan-ready taps go straight into the new plan overview. And ask "what does today's session look like?" — your coach now answers with a proper structured breakdown: warm-up, main set, cool-down, with the effort target for each block.

Kai Donovan is the new face on the roster: a Cycling Australia Level 2 coach out of New South Wales who treats the bike as one part of a bigger picture. Surf reports, swim sets, and the test cricket score sit alongside your interval session — for him it's all the same engine. Sun-warm, easy-going, no parody Aussie-isms.

Every coach now has a nationality and a list of languages they speak. Clara reads in English, Spanish, and Catalan. Lars in English, Danish, German. Sophie in French. Matteo in Italian. Elena across English, Spanish, Italian. A small language pill sits in the chat header — tap it, pick a language, and the coach drops a short welcome in the new language straight away ("¡Genial! A partir de ahora seguimos en español…") and keeps replying in that language from there. Per-coach memory, so swapping coaches doesn't lose your settings.

Home screen

Today, tomorrow, and your week — one screen, no scrolling

Your today's session is the first thing you see — name, distance, duration, effort, and a one-tap "View details" button. Tomorrow sits right beneath it. Below those, the next five days run in a compact horizontal scroll with session-type tags (Endurance, Intervals, Long Ride…) so the week reads as a real week, not two looming hero cards.

Your coach chat lives at the top of the screen so you're never more than a tap from a question. The "+ New plan" button shrunk to a small circle in the YOUR PLANS header — only the full pill version shows up when you don't have a plan yet, where it actually belongs.

Onboarding

You meet your coach the second you arrive

The onboarding tour fires the moment you finish signing in, before you see an empty home screen wondering what to do. Three steps, then you're in.

Picking a coach and choosing km or miles aren't optional any more — those two answers shape the rest of the app, so we ask once and remember forever. The Continue button stays disabled with a clear "Pick a coach to continue" hint until you've chosen.
