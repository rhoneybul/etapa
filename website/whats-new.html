A guide for getting started, a coach who knows your patch, and weekly check-ins that ask the right next question.

Getting started

An honest guide to what you actually need — and a tick-box for what you've already got

The first time you start the beginner programme, we now walk you through a Getting Started guide before plan generation. Bike, helmet, clothes, lights, tools, tech, food, routes, community — nine sections, each with three tiers from "the bare minimum" to "going all out". Charity shops and Marketplace get as much airtime as the big retailers, because half of getting into cycling is realising you don't need to spend much.

Up top there's a quick "what have you already got?" inventory — tap Got it on a helmet, a track pump, padded shorts. We use that everywhere downstream: the guide collapses sections you don't need, and your weekly coach tips skip nudges about kit you already own. Tap "Ask your coach about this" inside any section to drop straight into chat with a region-aware question pre-filled — bike-shop recommendations, gear advice, route apps. You can revisit the guide any time from Settings.

Weekly check-in

Your coach now opens with something useful, not just questions

The weekly check-in used to start with "five quick questions". Now it opens with a short note from your coach — two or three observations chosen from a hand-written catalog of 50+ tips that covers where you are on the journey, what's happening to your body, gear nudges, what races to watch, where to find routes, when to think about clubs, what to start eating on longer rides. Each tip rotates so you never see the same one twice; tap "Not for me" to opt out of a topic permanently.

Tips are tailored to your fitness level, how many weeks into your plan you are, what you've ticked off in the gear inventory, your country, and even the season — riders in the UK in November get winter-light nudges; riders anywhere in July get "the Tour is on this month, watch a stage."

Location-aware coach

Your coach knows your patch — and recommends accordingly

During onboarding we now ask where you're based (skippable; fifteen common countries plus "other"). The same field's editable in Settings. From there, the coach uses it everywhere it helps: equipment questions point at British Cycling, Tredz, Decathlon for UK riders; REI / Bikes Direct / RAGBRAI for US riders; 99 Bikes / Bowral Classic for Australian riders. Sportive nudges, club hunts, and event-watching tips all bias toward your region too.

Universal advice still leads — your country just adds the local colour. You can go off-grid by leaving location blank, and nothing breaks; you'll just get the global-only version.

Beginner programme

Pick a length that fits your life — same price either way

The Get into Cycling programme used to be locked at 12 weeks. Now you can pick 4, 6, 8, or 12 — same monthly subscription either way, no shorter-equals-cheaper trickery. We still recommend 12 (it's the sweet spot for actually building the habit) and the "RECOMMENDED" pill says so, but a 4-week toe-in-the-water works too. There's also a relaxed-pass card up front: "Miss a session? Fine. Skip a week? Also fine. Life happens."

After your plan is built, if you picked fewer than 12 weeks, you'll see a soft pink card on the plan-ready screen offering to extend to the recommended 12 — one tap, no rebuild from scratch. Original choice is shown so you always know what you're changing from.

Plan setup

Edit your regular ride or long-ride day without losing your place

On the Build Your Week step, the Edit links on the "regular ride" and "Long ride · SAT" pills used to yank you back through the wizard. Now they open a bottom-sheet drawer over the same screen. Regular rides drawer lists every recurring ride with edit + delete + add affordances; long-ride day drawer is a one-tap day picker that auto-dismisses. Done when you're done; the rest of your week stays exactly where you left it.

Same pattern as the language picker, the unavailability manager, and the plan-picker review edit — every drawer in the app now closes by tapping outside the surface, so cancel is always the easiest thing to do.

Calendar

"Tell your coach when you're unavailable" — with a proper manager

The unavailable-days pill used to read "Unavailable · 4" — a count that doubled as a status badge and confused more riders than it helped. Now the pill says "Tell your coach when you're unavailable" and tapping it opens a manager that lists every saved period as a card (date range, day count, reason, × to delete) with a dashed pink "+ Add another period" footer. New periods drop into the inline tap-start / tap-end range mode on the calendar.

Days you mark unavailable no longer show their would-have-been activity icon underneath. The dashed background and struck-through date are enough — listing what isn't happening was just noise.

Coach chat polish

Clear chat icon, no stuck "Opening…", coach reply badge clears as you tap

The "Clear" text in the chat header now wraps into the languages it actually translates to — "Limpiar" / "Löschen" weren't fitting. Replaced with a small trash icon; same accessibility label in every language. The unread "1" badge on the coach avatar now disappears the instant you tap to open chat, instead of waiting for the next focus refresh — and a follow-up read-state poll runs ~800ms later as a backstop.

The Send-to-trainer drawer now downloads fresh on every open instead of caching, so you'll never get the stuck "Opening…" state from a botched previous session. The export button itself is now hidden on outdoor rides — it only surfaces for indoor sessions where the .zwo file is actually useful.

Bugs squashed
  • Paywall dismissed after a fresh plan generation now lands on Home with a loading splash, instead of flashing the "Get started" empty state for half a second.
  • Weekly check-in step was being silently skipped for any rider with a coach already chosen in onboarding (basically everyone). Now reaches every rider.
  • Coach intro tips on the check-in are no longer overwritten by Claude's suggestions when the rider submits.
  • "Submit" on the activity feedback sheet is reachable above the keyboard now; multiline note has its own Done link, 500-character counter, and warns when within 50 of the cap.
  • Workout export no longer leaks the raw "writeAsStringAsync deprecated" message into the user-facing error. Friendlier copy, plus the deprecation itself is silenced.
  • Chat language welcome bubble survives a re-open of the chat — was being cleared along with the rest of state on every fresh mount.

Weekly check-in Updated

Two minutes on Sunday — your plan reshapes itself for next week, with what you said after each ride already in there.

Tap done after a ride, drop one line, the check-in already has it. Coach quotes you back when proposing changes.

Once a week your coach asks how things actually went. You tick what you did, drop one line about anything coming up ("can't ride Wednesday"), and the coach proposes concrete tweaks to next week — apply or skip each one with a tap.

Whatever you wrote after each ride this week is shown back to you in the check-in — pink "YOU SAID" pill under each session — so you don't retype the same thing. Your coach reads it as structured data and quotes you directly when suggesting tweaks ("you marked Tuesday way too hard" not "I see you struggled").

  • Tap done after any session and a quick popup asks effort, feel, and an optional note. Your coach replies on the spot with one short response, and you can jump into chat to dig in further.
  • Each session row shows its calendar date so you can place it in the week.
  • "Reschedule" pill in the header — push the check-in to a later day mid-week without skipping it entirely.
  • Pick the day and time in Settings → Weekly check-in.
  • Your coach never gives medical advice. Anything injury-related gets pointed at a physio, full stop.
Send to your trainer Updated

Save the workout file inside Etapa — read it, share it where you ride, no browser kick-out.

Tap, the file lands in Etapa, you see exactly what's in it, share to Zwift / Rouvy / Wahoo / Files when you're ready.

Open any structured ride and tap Send to trainer. We download a real .zwo workout file straight into Etapa — no browser, no Files-app dance. The modal shows exactly what's in your workout: warm-up, main set with target zones, cool-down, all with durations.

From there a single Share button opens the system share sheet on the local file. Pick Zwift, Rouvy, Wahoo SYSTM, intervals.icu, Files, AirDrop, Messages — whatever fits how you ride.

  • The file lives inside Etapa permanently. Re-share it any time without re-downloading.
  • Format auto-picked: .zwo for Zwift / Rouvy / intervals.icu, .fit for Wahoo SYSTM.
  • FTP set in your trainer app once → every workout plays back at the right watts.
Cycling type

Pick the bike that fits your riding — swap any session in one tap.

Road, gravel, MTB, e-bike, or indoor. Plan reads native to the bike you ride.

Pick the one that defines your training and the plan reads native to it (singletrack for MTB, mixed surface for gravel, structured indoor for trainer riders).

  • Tap the bike chip on any ride to swap just that session — same effort, sensible distance.
  • Changing every Thursday to gravel? It'll offer to apply across all upcoming weeks.
  • Change your default any time in Settings → Coaching → Default cycling type.
Calendar

Block out days, see coach changes before they apply.

Mark days unavailable when life intrudes. Coach proposals appear as a diff on the calendar — accept, review, or skip.

Heading away for work? Tap "Mark unavailable" in the calendar header, pick the days, drop a one-line reason. Hatched cells make it obvious those days are off-limits, and the planner routes around them.

When your coach proposes changes for next week, they show as a diff on the calendar — added sessions in pink with a left border, moves marked "MOVED FROM SAT", removals struck through. Apply all in one tap, review them one-by-one, or skip.

  • "See on calendar" from a coach chat message lands you on the exact date the change applies to, day-detail panel already open.
  • Holds work travel, family weekends, scheduled rest — all handled the same way.
