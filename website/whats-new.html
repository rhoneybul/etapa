A clearer way to pick your plan, drag-and-drop sessions, a chat that never drops a message — and a brand-new coach.

Choosing your plan

A plan built around where you actually are — in under a minute

Three quick questions: what are you training for, how long is your current longest ride, and when's your event. That's it. No FTP test, no jargon, no "beginner / intermediate / advanced" guesswork. Your actual longest ride becomes the anchor for Week 1, so your plan starts at a distance you can genuinely complete and builds up from there.

On the training-types and build-your-week steps you'll see a small "we typically recommend" card — and the recommendation adapts to your fitness level and the session types you've already chosen. After your answers, a review screen lays out every choice as a tap-to-edit row — no scrolling back through the flow, no starting over, and going back from any later step actually goes back.

Drag and drop

Move a session straight from home or the calendar

Press-and-hold any session on Home and drag it to a new day. Or open Calendar, press-and-hold any cell that already has a ride on it, and drag the session right out of the grid — no need to scroll down to the day list to pick it up first.

Same gesture, same press-and-hold timing in both places. If you move a session to a day before Week 1 starts, we honour the move without re-generating the rest of the plan around it.

Messaging

A chat that opens instantly and never loses a reply

We rewrote how coach chat is stored end-to-end. Open the screen and your conversation is right there from the cache, then quietly reconciles with the server in the background. Send a message and close the app mid-reply — when you come back, your message and the coach's reply are both intact. If a send fails, a small inline resend arrow appears inside the bubble, exactly where you'd expect to tap.

Push notifications now drop you on the exact message that triggered them, not the top of the chat. Plan-ready taps go straight into the new plan overview. And ask "what does today's session look like?" — your coach now answers with a proper structured breakdown: warm-up, main set, cool-down, with the effort target for each block.

New coach & languages

Meet Kai — and chat with your coach in their language

Kai Donovan is the new face on the roster: a Cycling Australia Level 2 coach out of New South Wales who treats the bike as one part of a bigger picture. Surf reports, swim sets, and the test cricket score sit alongside your interval session — for him it's all the same engine. Sun-warm, easy-going, no parody Aussie-isms.

Every coach now has a nationality and a list of languages they speak. Clara reads in English, Spanish, and Catalan. Lars in English, Danish, German. Sophie in French. Matteo in Italian. Elena across English, Spanish, Italian. A small language pill sits in the chat header — tap it, pick a language, and the coach drops a short welcome in the new language straight away ("¡Genial! A partir de ahora seguimos en español…") and keeps replying in that language from there. Per-coach memory, so swapping coaches doesn't lose your settings.

Home screen

Today, tomorrow, and your week — one screen, no scrolling

Your today's session is the first thing you see — name, distance, duration, effort, and a one-tap "View details" button. Tomorrow sits right beneath it. Below those, the next five days run in a compact horizontal scroll with session-type tags (Endurance, Intervals, Long Ride…) so the week reads as a real week, not two looming hero cards.

Your coach chat lives at the top of the screen so you're never more than a tap from a question. The "+ New plan" button shrunk to a small circle in the YOUR PLANS header — only the full pill version shows up when you don't have a plan yet, where it actually belongs.

Onboarding

You meet your coach the second you arrive

The onboarding tour fires the moment you finish signing in, before you see an empty home screen wondering what to do. Three steps, then you're in.

Picking a coach and choosing km or miles aren't optional any more — those two answers shape the rest of the app, so we ask once and remember forever. The Continue button stays disabled with a clear "Pick a coach to continue" hint until you've chosen.

Bugs squashed
  • User messages no longer disappear after a follow-up — server now uses the live conversation as the baseline before appending the coach's reply.
  • Coach replies no longer vanish on return — a server/client race that overwrote the completed reply with an empty one is fixed on both sides.
  • Coach replies stop arriving as bare commitments — "I'll change that" without an actual change is now blocked.
  • Coach no longer answers its own questions with a boilerplate refusal — context of the prior turn is included in the on-topic check.
  • Onboarding tour now fires for genuinely new accounts, not just ones with at least one plan.
  • Trial banner now appears for both trialing and starter states, with a sensible fallback when the period-end date is missing.
  • Tapping a day on Home reliably scrolls the matching row into view — we measure at click time now instead of relying on stale layout values.
  • Android keyboard no longer hides the coach chat input.
  • Calendar chat input lifts above the keyboard on Android, instead of being hidden by it.
  • Splash screen on iOS and Android no longer shows the old "ETAPA / train with purpose" text — both now match the minimal in-app loader.
  • Race lookup keeps the correct distance when you change the event.
  • The Delete Plan crash (black screen) is fixed — now shows a proper loading overlay.
  • Plan Overview chart uses distinct colours for endurance vs tempo.
  • Generate Plan no longer crashes on the longest-ride step.
  • Plans with a Peak phase no longer flag three weeks in a row as "Peak week" — only the actual highest-volume week gets the label.
  • Back from plan config returns to your previous step instead of dropping you on Home.
  • Bottom buttons no longer get clipped by the Android 3-button nav bar on Welcome / plan picker / onboarding screens.
  • The "tap a session" hint moved under the divider so it clearly refers to the day list, not the week strip above it.
