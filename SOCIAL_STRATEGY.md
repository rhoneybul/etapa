## The one-page version

You have two raw materials:

1. A 20-minute founder video ("Hello Etapa") that is genuinely charming, honest, and specific. It's your secret weapon.
2. 12 voiced blog posts, each anchored to a real story (dad, mum, girlfriend, Marrakesh, Devon, Girona, Wimbledon Common).

You're going to clip the video into ~18 short reels, pair it with behind-the-scenes of building Etapa and photos of real riding, and land roughly 3–4 posts a week on Instagram and TikTok for 12 weeks. Every post drives one place: the register-interest form.

The tone you're chasing is the tone of the video. Casual. Self-deprecating. Specific. Not performative. "I enjoy cycling. I won't go as far as saying I'm a cyclist." That's the voice. Every single post should sound like it came out of your mouth, not a marketing deck.

---

## Voice principles (pulled directly from the video)

Everything you post has to pass these seven filters:

1. **75% well, 25% poorly.** The first line you say on camera is "My ride went 75% well, 25% poorly." Keep it. Every post that's a flex should contain a small admission of something going wrong.
2. **"I'm not a cyclist, I just cycle."** Never position yourself — or Etapa — as for "proper cyclists." You're a software engineer who loves the sport. That's the whole angle.
3. **Specific beats generic.** "Lost in Wimbledon Common, nearly fell off at 2km/h" > "Had a funny ride." Names, places, moments.
4. **One anchor per post.** Dad's Marrakesh trip. Mum's LEJOG. Girlfriend's birthday. France pizza. Paris-Roubaix. Use one. Don't stack them.
5. **Light on the jargon.** You can mention lycra, gears, clips, but treat it like a second language you're still learning. The moment you sound like a pro, you're off-voice.
6. **"Cycling can mean anything for anyone."** This is the north star sentence. It's in the video. It's the brand. Every post should pass the test: does this expand cycling or gatekeep it?
7. **Probably a disaster, but could be fun.** The bikepacking-with-a-surfboard energy. Curious, amused, honest about not having it figured out.

Banned words: journey (in the corporate sense), crushing it, unlock your potential, game-changer, beast mode, next-level, grind (unless self-deprecating about your day job).

Preferred: rolled out, spun around, faffed about, hammered, bonked, pub stop, cafe, proper, unreal.

## Channel strategy

Priority 1: **Instagram** (primary). Reels for reach, static/carousel for brand, stories for register-interest pushes.

Priority 2: **TikTok / YouTube Shorts**. Every Instagram Reel should cross-post here. Same caption, adjusted hashtags.

Priority 3: **Twitter / X**. Text-first. Builder community is there. 2–3 posts/week, more during launch week.

Priority 4: **LinkedIn**. Once a week. Founder-building angle. Different audience — fewer hashtags, more reflection, same stories.

Priority 5: **YouTube (long form)**. Publish the "Hello Etapa" video publicly at start of Week 1 as the anchor. Maybe one more 5-minute update at Week 6 and a launch video at Week 10.

Don't bother with Threads until after launch. Don't bother with Facebook at all unless running paid.

## Caption formulas (steal these)

When you're on a train and need to write a caption fast, use one of these. Fill in the blanks.

**The confession:**
> [Specific honest thing that went wrong]. [What you did anyway]. [How that connects to Etapa / cycling].

Example: "Lost in Wimbledon Common today for the 25th time. Nearly fell off at 2km/h. Had the best ride I've had in weeks anyway. Cycling is like that sometimes."

**The one-story:**
> [Anchor] — [dad/mum/girlfriend/friend specific moment]. [Why it shaped me]. [Link to Etapa].

Example: "Halfway up the hill last summer, my girlfriend said 'I know what I want for my birthday.' That's why Etapa exists."

**The problem statement:**
> [Thing cycling culture does badly]. [Why it's a problem]. [What Etapa does about it].

Example: "Every cycling app asks for your FTP on page 2. Most new cyclists don't know what FTP is. That's a product problem, and it's what Etapa fixes."

**The blog hook:**
> [One-sentence story from the blog]. [One-sentence takeaway]. Full piece in bio.

Example: "My dad rode from Marrakesh into the Atlas Mountains in a t-shirt. You need less kit than you think. Full piece in bio."

**The question:**
> [Specific provocative question]. [Your take, two sentences]. [CTA].

Example: "Are you cycling, or are you a cyclist? Honestly I think the first is more fun. That's who Etapa is for. Link in bio."

## Weekly rhythm

Wake up on Sunday night. 45 minutes max. Following routine:

- **Review the week that's ending** — 10 min. Screenshot top 3 posts for reference, kill any scheduled ones that no longer fit.
- **Batch-write next week's captions** — 20 min. Use this document + formulas.
- **Queue in Later / Buffer / Meta Business Suite** — 10 min.
- **Tap reply a few comments from the past week** — 5 min. Don't ghost.

Daily rhythm (Mon–Fri, 15 min):
- Morning: reply to DMs and comments from yesterday's posts.
- Evening: one story. Never forced. If nothing's happening, skip.

On a ride day: take one photo. Just one. That's your content for the week if you need it.

## Hashtag strategy (nuance)

**Do:**
- Mix hashtag sizes. 2–3 big (>500k posts), 4–6 medium (10k–500k), 3–5 niche (<10k). Niche hashtags are where you get found.
- Put hashtags in the first comment, not the caption. Cleaner look.
- Refresh your set every 4 weeks. Algorithm penalises identical hashtag blocks.

**Don't:**
- Use #ad #sponsored unless you actually are. Triggers review.
- Use location hashtags that aren't true (#CyclingLondon when you're in Edinburgh).
- Stuff 30 tags. IG limits to 30 but performance peaks at 10–15.

## Community engagement rules

For every post:

- **Reply to every comment in the first 2 hours.** Even with a heart. This is the single biggest thing for reach.
- **Save other people's good cycling content to IG saves** daily. Signals to algorithm what you're about.
- **Comment on 5 accounts in your niche, daily.** Real comments, not "great post!". 5 minutes.

For DMs:
- If someone messages with a real question, reply properly, even if it's long.
- Share the blog post for free. Don't gate educational content behind the app yet.

## What to measure (keep it simple)

Once a week, on Sunday night, track four numbers in a spreadsheet:

1. **Instagram followers** — growth rate matters more than total
2. **Register-interest signups** — the only metric that actually counts pre-launch
3. **Top-performing post of the week** — why did it work? Was it a YouTube clip? Blog? Brand?
4. **Open rate of the weekly email** — if you're sending one

Post-launch, add:

5. App installs
6. Trial → paid conversion
7. Top install source (organic IG, paid IG, direct, App Store search)

Don't look at engagement rate obsessively. Good voice + right audience produces good engagement eventually. Focus on list growth and installs.

## What NOT to post

- Pure screenshots of feature lists. Always anchor to a story or person.
- Anything implying you're "one of the top cyclists." You'll lose the audience.
- Performative inclusivity posts (International Women's Day with a stock photo). Only post something if you have a genuine, specific, personal story to tell.
- Reposts of other cycling brands' content. Your stuff only.
- Before-and-after body photos. Not the brand.
- Negative takes on competitors by name. Punch up (at "the industry", "cycling culture") not down (at Strava, TrainerRoad).

## Emergency content bank (when life happens)

If you've had a bad week and have nothing queued, use one of these in 10 minutes:

1. Post a screenshot of your latest ride (Strava or app) with one-sentence commentary.
2. Screenshot a good DM conversation (with permission) and caption "This is why I'm building Etapa."
3. Repost a clip you already made 6+ weeks ago with a new caption.
4. Ask a question in a static post: "What do you wish you'd known in your first year of cycling? Collecting for a blog post."
5. Share someone else's ride: "[Name] is doing something brilliant — go give them a follow."

## Final reminder

Every post passes this one test before you publish it:

**Does this sound like me? Would I say this in a pub?**

If yes, post it.
If no, rewrite it until you would.

That's the whole strategy.
